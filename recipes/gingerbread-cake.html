Gingerbread Cake with Lemon Glaze

Description

This is a gingerbread cake recipe from the New York Times that I have been making every holiday season for a few years now. It has become a classic in my family and we always look forward to it during Christmas time. The recipe is a little involved, but altogether can be done in a few hours. It's been a hard recipe to mess up, even when it feels like it won't turn out.

Here's the link to the orignal recipe on NY Times: Moist Gingerbread Cake with Lemon Glaze

Ingredients

  • Butter for greasing
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons cinnamon
  • 1/8 teaspoons nutmeg, preferably fresh
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup, packed, dark brown sugar
  • 3 tablespoons turbinado sugar
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons finely grated fresh ginger (a 2-ounce piece, peeled)
  • Grated zest of 2 lemons
  • 1/2 cup grapeseed oil
  • 1 large egg
  • 3/4 cup stout beer
  • 3/4 cup molasses
  • 1/3 cup brewed coffee
  • 3/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup confectioner's surgar, sifted
  • 2 to 4 tablespoons lemon juice

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees.
  2. Grease an 8-inch springform cake pan and line bottom with parchment paper.
  3. In a bowl, whisk flour, cocoa, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg and Â½ teaspoon salt. Set aside.
  4. In a large bowl, combine brown sugar, turbinado, ginger, zest of 1 lemon and oil. Beat in egg.
  5. Place beer, molasses and coffee in a deep pot, at least 3 quarts. Bring to a boil.
  6. Remove from heat and stir in baking soda. Mixture will bubble up. Allow to cool 5 minutes.
  7. Whisk flour mixture alternately with beer mixture into brown sugar mixture, in 3 shifts.
  8. Pour batter in pan and bake 40 minutes without opening oven.
  9. Give pan a 180-degree turn and bake another 15 minutes, until cake is springy and a tester comes out clean.
  10. Cool on a rack. When cool, remove sides of pan, invert briefly to remove bottom of pan and paper and set upright on a plate.
  11. Place confectioner's sugar in a bowl, whisk in remaining zest and salt, then lemon juice (add up to 2 additional tablespoons to moisten if necessary).
  12. Spread over top of cake, allowing glaze to drip down the sides.
